About Matthew

Bridging the gap between technical implementation and executive decision-making

Matthew P. Jennings is a cybersecurity architect, instructor, and thought leader with over 15 years of experience helping organizations strengthen their security posture across government, financial, and enterprise sectors. Known for bridging the gap between technical implementation and executive decision-making, Matthew brings clarity and strategy to an often-complex security landscape.

As an Information Security Architect in the Financial Sector, he leads security architecture and AI adoption initiatives for critical infrastructure and globally-influential organizations. His work focuses on aligning technical safeguards with organizational risk strategies, ensuring that cybersecurity decisions are both actionable and aligned with business goals.

Beyond the enterprise, Matthew is a passionate educator who teaches DHS and FEMA-sponsored cybersecurity courses through the National Cybersecurity Preparedness Consortium. He also mentors emerging leaders as a former mentor in the MS-ISAC Leadership Mentoring Program and served as a member of the Center for Internet Security (CIS) SLTT Vendor Advisory Board, advising on vendor collaboration and best practices for state, local, and tribal territory governments.

Dedicated to advancing the profession and evangelizing the security industry, Matthew's mission is to empower organizations and individuals to make risk-informed, data-driven security decisions ‚Äî connecting the "why" of executive strategy with the "how" of technical implementation.
