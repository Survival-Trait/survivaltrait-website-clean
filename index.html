About SurvivalTrait

SurvivalTrait is a public knowledge project focused on cybersecurity, risk, and decision-making in service of a stronger, more informed security community.

The name comes from a line in the movie Hackers:

"Hacking isn't just a crime. It's a survival trait."

Most people hear that line as a clever quote.

Some people recognize their entire life in it.

To most of society, the term "hacking" still conjures a narrow stereotype. A hoodie-wearing criminal in a dark room, breaking into federal mainframes or pilfering bank accounts. In practice, hacking is far more visceral and far less nefarious than that.

It's what happens when someone refuses to accept the world at face value. When curiosity becomes instinct. When understanding how things work becomes the difference between moving forward and being left behind.

At its core, hacking is not about crime.

It's about resilience.

It's about curiosity.

It's about questioning what's in front of you, asking "what if," and finding a way through when assumptions fail.

That mindset is the foundation of SurvivalTrait.

This site is where I share lessons learned from a literal lifetime spent surrounded by hackers, phreakers, software pirates, and geeks--forged by a career of more than 25 years in the IT and Security fields across the private sector, financial services, government, and critical infrastructure‚Äîhoned by teaching and mentoring students and practitioners at different stages of their careers. The focus is not on products, certifications, or trends. It's on how to think clearly about systems, risk, and responsibility in the real world.

SurvivalTrait is not a gated training platform or a content funnel. It's an open collection of essays, field notes, and practical insights intended to make security knowledge universally accessible.

When access to knowledge is restricted to only those who can afford it, availability has already failed--and systems built without knowledge are not secure.

If you have ever relied on curiosity, creativity, or resourcefulness to navigate complexity, you already understand what this project is about‚Äîand why you're here.
